# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two overlapping groups, both using the app in a private, deliberate moment:

- **Active job seekers**: preparing to talk to recruiters or write applications. They need to excavate and articulate their professional story clearly and quickly.
- **Career reflectors**: not actively hunting, but pausing to understand themselves — seeking self-knowledge, clarity, or a foundation for coaching conversations.

Both are intelligent adults who want honesty, not flattery. They open the app alone, thoughtfully. The primary task on any screen is either: answering questions in the interview, reading the insight document, or exporting a profile.

## Product Purpose

Persona is a private, local-first AI-powered tool that conducts a structured career interview and produces two outputs: a private self-knowledge document (Insight) and a shareable professional website (Profile). All data and API keys stay on the device. The product succeeds when a user walks away with a clearer, more honest picture of who they are professionally — one they feel ready to talk about.

## Brand Personality

Thoughtful, private, empowering.

The voice is direct and unhurried. It respects the user's intelligence and doesn't over-explain. It has warmth without being cheerful; confidence without being slick. Think: a trusted mentor who asks good questions and lets the answers land.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS aesthetics**: indigo-gradient heroes, big-number metrics, floating-card grids, glowing CTAs. The product should feel nothing like a VC-funded dashboard.
- **Overly corporate / formal**: navy blues, rigid grid layouts, "professional" in the suit-and-tie sense. This is personal, not institutional.
- **Social media / LinkedIn vibes**: performative, engagement-optimized, public-first. Persona is the opposite: private, inward-facing, honest.

## Design Principles

1. **Private by posture**: The UI should feel like a closed notebook, not a public profile. Nothing about the layout should invite performance or comparison.
2. **Warmth through restraint**: Encouragement comes from clarity and focus, not color or animation. A calm, unhurried interface is the warmth.
3. **Trust through honesty**: Every label, prompt, and piece of copy tells the truth. No upsells, no dark patterns, no fake urgency.
4. **The work is the content**: The interview and the outputs are the product. Chrome, decoration, and UI furniture should recede until needed.
5. **One device, one person**: Design for a single user in a quiet moment — not for demos, screenshots, or sharing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum across all surfaces. Keyboard navigation throughout. Sufficient color contrast on all text (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text). Reduced-motion preference respected for any animations added.
